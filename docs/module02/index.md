# Module 2 — Hydraulic Actuation: How the Machine Pushes

Module 1 told us *how long* each leg must be. This module is about the device that
actually changes that length — the **hydraulic cylinder** — and the physics that
turns oil pressure and flow into force and motion. We'll meet the one quirk that
shapes everything (the area asymmetry φ), the square-root valve flow law that
surprises everyone, and how a platform load becomes pressure in each cylinder through
the Jacobian. By the end you can size a cylinder, a valve, and a power unit.

## What you'll be able to do

- Explain how a cylinder converts pressure to force and flow to speed.
- Compute the cap and rod areas and the asymmetry ratio φ.
- Predict a cylinder's four defining numbers: extend/retract force and speed.
- Apply the valve flow law and explain why flow scales with √(pressure drop).
- Trace how a platform load becomes cylinder pressure through the Jacobian.
- Size a pump for flow and a relief valve for pressure.
